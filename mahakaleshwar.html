About Mahakaleshwar Jyotirlinga

The Mahakaleshwar Jyotirlinga is one of the most famous Hindu temples dedicated to Lord Shiva and is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas, shrines which are said to be the most sacred abodes of Shiva. It is located in the ancient city of Ujjain in the state of Madhya Pradesh, India.

Significance

The idol of Mahakaleshwar is known to be dakshinamurti, which means that it is facing the south. This is a unique feature, upheld by the tantric shivnetra tradition to be found only in Mahakaleshwar among the 12 Jyotirlingas.

Bhasma Aarti

The Bhasma Aarti, a ritual involving the offering of sacred ash to the deity, is a renowned and awe-inspiring ceremony that draws thousands of devotees daily. It takes place in the early hours of the morning before sunrise.
